StrongRow — strength rowing, measured to a tenth of a stroke per minute.

StrongRow is a rowing app built for slow, powerful strokes. If you row long, low-rate pieces for strength and durability — 14 to 20 strokes per minute, full pressure, long recovery — you have probably noticed that your watch's native rowing cadence falls apart exactly where your training lives: it is integer-only, and below roughly 18–20 spm the built-in stroke counter can miss strokes entirely or show zero. StrongRow was written to fix that.

HOW IT WORKS

StrongRow reads the watch's raw accelerometer at 25 Hz and detects every stroke itself. The signal is band-pass filtered on the dominant axis, an autocorrelation locks onto your true stroke-cycle period, and each catch/drive surge is timed against that lock — which rejects the recovery surge, non-rowing hand motion, and phantom readings. Stroke rate comes from the actual time between drives, shown to a tenth of a stroke per minute — 16.4 spm, not "16" — with no artificial low-rate floor.

DRIVES, NOT BLADE MOVEMENTS

On open water you take small corrective strokes — steering taps that keep the boat tracking in wind and chop. The native counter registers every blade movement; StrongRow's period lock counts only true drives. In a windy 5 x 4:00 field test the app counted 66–68 drives on every single rep while native cadence counted 89–109 blade movements, and distance per drive stroke was stable to 3.7% across the set against 22% per blade movement. For stroke-length and durability analysis, the drive count is the physically meaningful unit — and the gap between the two counts is your boat-handling workload.

A COMPLETE SESSION IN THE FIT FILE

GPS is on for the whole row: position, speed and distance are recorded, with the live /500 m split and metres per stroke on screen. Developer fields log row_stroke_rate, dist_per_stroke, corrective-stroke rate, raw R-R intervals and rolling rMSSD — beat-to-beat HRV captured explicitly, regardless of the watch's Log HRV setting.

BUILT-IN INTERVAL WORKOUT

Out of the box: 5 x 4:00 of work at 16–18 spm with 2:00 of rest, and a press-START gate after each rest so the next interval begins only when you are ready. An untimed warmup and cooldown wrap the intervals, each ended by a START press, so launching and docking are captured on their own laps without eating into the work.

During work intervals the large stroke-rate number is your coach: GREEN inside the target band, ORANGE outside it. Every transition vibrates and beeps, and each step is its own lap for effortless interval-by-interval review.

Everything is adjustable from Garmin Connect app settings — no rebuild:
- Interval workout on/off (off = free row)
- Number of intervals, work and rest length
- Target band, low and high (spm)
- Press-START gate after rest on/off
- Warmup and cooldown steps on/off

Prefer unstructured? Turn the workout off and StrongRow is a clean free-row recorder: live tenths-precision stroke rate, START to record, BACK to save.

CONTROLS

- START/STOP: begin the workout, end warmup/cooldown, pause/resume, continue past a rest gate
- BACK: save the row and exit

WHY A WATCH APP (AND NOT A DATA FIELD)

Connect IQ does not allow data fields to read the raw high-frequency accelerometer, so this cannot be a field inside the native rowing activity. As a watch app, StrongRow reads the 25 Hz stream and records its own complete session from the wrist — no external pods required.

WHO IT IS FOR

- Rowers and scullers doing low-rate strength work, technique paddles, or steady-state at full pressure
- Anyone who wants honest sub-integer cadence and true drive counts
- Self-coached athletes who want each interval on its own lap with the real stroke rate in the file

StrongRow — strength rowing, measured to a tenth of a stroke per minute.

StrongRow is a rowing app built for slow, powerful strokes. If you row long, low-rate pieces for strength and durability — 14 to 20 strokes per minute, full pressure, long recovery — you have probably noticed that your watch's native rowing cadence falls apart exactly where your training lives: it is integer-only, and below roughly 18–20 spm the built-in stroke counter can miss strokes entirely or show zero. StrongRow was written to fix that.

HOW IT WORKS

Instead of relying on the native cadence, StrongRow reads the watch's raw accelerometer at 25 Hz and detects every stroke itself. The acceleration signal is band-pass filtered to strip out gravity drift and hand jitter, then each catch/drive surge is picked out with an adaptive-envelope threshold, hysteresis, and a refractory window that rejects the smaller recovery peak. Your stroke rate is computed from the actual time between strokes and shown to a tenth of a stroke per minute — 16.4 spm, not "16" — with no artificial low-rate floor. Slow, deliberate strokes are resolved cleanly all the way down.

The computed rate is also written into the FIT file as a developer field (row_stroke_rate), so you can analyze your true low-rate cadence after the session in Garmin Connect or any FIT-aware tool.

BUILT-IN INTERVAL WORKOUT

StrongRow ships with a configurable low-rate interval session. Out of the box: 5 x 4:00 of work at 16–18 spm with 2:00 of rest, and a press-START gate after each rest so the next interval begins only when you are actually ready — no scrambling back onto the seat because a timer ran out.

During work intervals the large stroke-rate number is your coach: it turns GREEN when you are inside the target band and ORANGE when you drift outside it. Every work/rest transition vibrates and beeps, and each step is recorded as its own lap in the FIT file, so interval-by-interval review afterwards is effortless.

Everything is adjustable from Garmin Connect app settings — no rebuild, no fuss:
- Interval workout on/off (off = free row)
- Number of intervals
- Work length (minutes)
- Rest length (minutes)
- Target band, low and high (spm)
- Press-START gate after rest on/off

Prefer an unstructured session? Turn the workout off and StrongRow becomes a clean free-row recorder: live tenths-precision stroke rate, START to record, BACK to save.

CONTROLS

- START/STOP: begin the workout, pause/resume, continue past a rest gate
- BACK: save the row and exit

WHY A WATCH APP (AND NOT A DATA FIELD)

Connect IQ does not allow data fields to access the raw high-frequency accelerometer, so this cannot be done as a field inside Garmin's native rowing activity. As a watch app, StrongRow can read the 25 Hz sensor stream and records its own complete rowing session. It uses the watch's accelerometer on your wrist — external straps and pods are not required (and their accelerometers are not accessible to Connect IQ anyway).

WHO IT IS FOR

- Rowers and scullers doing low-rate strength work, technique paddles, or steady-state at full pressure
- Indoor rowers who want honest sub-integer cadence instead of a rounded guess
- Coaches and self-coached athletes who want each interval on its own lap with the real stroke rate in the file

StrongRow does one thing carefully: it tells you your true stroke rate when you are rowing slow and strong, and it keeps you honest inside your target band. Created by Stephen Cieply, PhD. Open source under the MIT License.
